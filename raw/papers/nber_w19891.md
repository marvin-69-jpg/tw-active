# Scale and Skill in Active Management — PSTZ (2014 NBER WP)

## Abstract（原文）

We empirically analyze the nature of returns to scale in active mutual fund
management. We find strong evidence of decreasing returns at the industry
level: As the size of the active mutual fund industry increases, a fund's
ability to outperform passive benchmarks declines. At the fund level, all
methods considered indicate decreasing returns, but estimates that avoid
econometric biases are insignificant. We also find that the active management
industry has become more skilled over time. This upward trend in skill
coincides with industry growth, which precludes the skill improvement from
boosting fund performance. Finally, we find that performance deteriorates over
a typical fund's lifetime. This result can also be explained by industry-level
decreasing returns to scale.

## 重點 takeaways（精讀後筆記）

1. **兩個 hypothesis 拆開**
   - Fund-level decreasing returns（Berk-Green 2004 路線）
   - Industry-level decreasing returns（PSTZ 自己 2012 提的，本篇首次實證）

2. **Fund-level 證據其實弱**
   - OLS 有兩種 bias：skill 是 omitted variable + Stambaugh (1999) bias
   - 用 recursive demeaning（Moon-Phillips, Hjalmarsson）消 bias 後，fund-level 估計**統計上不顯著**
   - 本篇對 CHHK (2004) 的方法論 rebut

3. **Industry-level 證據強且 robust**
   - 是這個 hypothesis 的**首篇實證**
   - high-turnover、high-volatility、small-cap funds 上效果特別強——擁擠交易直覺一致

4. **「skill 上升但 performance 不變」這個關鍵 framing**
   - 1979 平均 24bp/月 → 2011 平均 42bp/月，新 fund 比老 fund skilled
   - 但平均 alpha 沒上升 → industry-level diseconomy 把進步吃完
   - Quote：「the active management industry today is bigger and more competitive...
     it takes more skill just to keep up with the rest of the pack」

5. **年輕 fund 贏老 fund**
   - 3 年內 vs 10 年以上，年化差 ~0.9%（gross benchmark-adjusted）
   - 同樣是 industry-level diseconomy 的 implication

## 樣本

- 1979–2011，3,126 檔 actively managed U.S. equity mutual funds
- CRSP + Morningstar 對齊（建在 Berk-Binsbergen 2012 之上）
